Dennis Ritchie

A timeline of Dennis Ritchie's life

  1. 1941 - Born in Bronxville, New York
  2. 1968 - Attended Harvard University and obtained his Ph.D.
  3. 1970 - Helped create early versions of the UNIX operating system, along with this colleagues Ken Thompson and Brian Kernighan.
  4. 1972 - Started to improve on Thompson's B programming language, subsequently creating the C programming language.
  5. 1978 - Co-authored The C Programming Language with Brian Kernighan (A.K.A "K&R").
  6. 1983 - Received a Turing Award for his contributions to UNIX and C.
  7. 1997 - Made a Fellow of the Computer History Museum for his contributions to UNIX and C.
  8. 1999 - Received the National Medal of Technology jointly with Thompson for his contributions to UNIX and C.

"UNIX is basically a simple operating system, but you have to be a genius to understand the simplicity."
